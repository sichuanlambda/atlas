## Detailed City Notes

### Tier 1 ‚Äî Strong Candidates

**New York City** ‚Äî The obvious giant. Massive architectural diversity (Colonial, Federal, Beaux-Arts, Art Deco, International Style, Postmodern, contemporary). r/nyc is one of the largest city subs (~500k+). However: **extremely competitive SEO**. Dozens of existing architecture tour guides, TimeOut, Architectural Digest, AIA guides, dedicated walking tour companies. Hard to rank. High traffic ceiling but steep climb.

**Washington DC** ‚Äî Enormous Commons coverage (1.6M results ‚Äî likely inflated by "Washington" matching broadly, but still excellent). Neoclassical, Beaux-Arts, Brutalist (FBI HQ, HUD), modern (National Museum of African American History). Strong tourism (20M+ annual visitors). SEO competition moderate ‚Äî most guides focus on monuments, not architecture specifically. Good gap opportunity for an architecture-focused guide.

**Philadelphia** ‚Äî Underrated architectural powerhouse. Colonial/Federal (Independence Hall), Victorian (Rittenhouse), Art Deco (PSFS Building ‚Äî first International Style skyscraper in US), Brutalist (city hall environs), contemporary (Comcast buildings). 16K Commons images. **Strong SEO gap** ‚Äî fewer dedicated architecture guides than NYC/DC/Chicago. r/philadelphia is active (~400k). HABS has extensive Philadelphia coverage.

**Boston** ‚Äî Colonial, Federal, Richardsonian Romanesque (Trinity Church), Back Bay Victorian, modern (City Hall ‚Äî famous Brutalist). 115K Commons images. Good tourism. But: reasonably well-served by existing architecture guides (Boston Architectural College, AIA Boston, walking tours).

**New Orleans** ‚Äî Highly distinctive architecture (French Colonial, Creole, Shotgun houses, Garden District mansions, Italianate, Cast-iron galleries). **Extremely recognizable and photogenic.** Strong tourism (18M+ annual). **Good SEO gap** ‚Äî most guides focus on food/music/nightlife, architecture coverage is secondary. 4.8K Commons images is moderate but the HABS collection for New Orleans/Louisiana is extensive.

### Tier 2 ‚Äî Solid Options

**Los Angeles** ‚Äî Case Study Houses, Googie, Spanish Colonial Revival, Art Deco (Eastern Columbia), Mid-Century Modern, contemporary (Disney Hall, Broad). Huge tourism. But sprawling city makes a "guide" harder. Very competitive SEO.

**Miami** ‚Äî Art Deco Historic District is world-famous. MiMo (Miami Modern). Contemporary skyline. Good tourism. But architecturally narrow compared to older cities ‚Äî essentially Art Deco + Contemporary.

**Detroit** ‚Äî Fascinating story: Beaux-Arts grandeur (Michigan Central, Guardian Building, Fisher Building), Art Deco, industrial ruins, contemporary renewal. **Excellent SEO opportunity** ‚Äî architecture tourism in Detroit is a growing niche, underserved online. Lower tourism volume is the drawback. Strong narrative hook ("ruin porn to renaissance").

**Savannah** ‚Äî Gorgeous city with unique urban plan (Oglethorpe squares). Federal, Greek Revival, Victorian, Gothic. **Very underserved for architecture guides online** ‚Äî most content is generic "pretty squares" tourism. Only 480 Commons images is a concern, but HABS covers Savannah well. Low tourism volume relative to major cities.

### Tier 3 ‚Äî Lower Priority

**Pittsburgh** ‚Äî Good architecture (H.H. Richardson, Fallingwater nearby, Steel-era industrial, Andy Warhol Bridge district) but limited tourism draw and small image pool.

**Seattle** ‚Äî Some notable buildings (Space Needle, Central Library, Smith Tower) but not primarily an architecture destination. Mid-range everything.

**Portland OR** ‚Äî Eclectic but not architecturally distinctive enough. Limited landmark density.

**Nashville** ‚Äî Growing city but architecture is not its draw. Parthenon replica is fun but niche.

**Austin** ‚Äî State Capitol is notable but overall architectural scene is thin for a guide.

## SEO Gap Analysis

**Best opportunities (high demand, low competition):**

1. **New Orleans** ‚Äî People search for NOLA constantly but architecture-specific content is drowned out by food/music. An authoritative architecture guide would stand out.
2. **Philadelphia** ‚Äî Surprising gap. Historic and diverse architecture but few dedicated online architecture guides. "Philadelphia architecture tour" has competition mainly from local walking tour companies, not content sites.
3. **Detroit** ‚Äî Growing interest in "Detroit architecture" driven by ruin tourism and revival narrative. Very few comprehensive guides exist.
4. **Savannah** ‚Äî Niche but passionate audience. "Savannah architecture" searches exist but content is shallow.

**Worst opportunities (saturated):**
- NYC, Chicago (already have), Boston, LA ‚Äî heavily covered by existing guides.

## üèÜ RECOMMENDATION: Top 2 Cities

### 1. New York City
**Reasoning:** Despite fierce SEO competition, NYC is simply too large to ignore. It has:
- The highest search volume for architecture-related queries of any US city
- Unmatched architectural diversity (300+ years of building styles)
- Massive Reddit community for organic distribution
- Excellent image availability
- 60M+ annual tourists

The key is differentiation: focus on hidden gems, neighborhood-by-neighborhood coverage, and lesser-known buildings that existing guides skip. The sheer traffic volume means even a small % capture is significant.

### 2. New Orleans
**Reasoning:** This is the high-upside pick. New Orleans offers:
- **Unique architectural identity** unlike any other US city (French/Spanish Colonial, Creole, Shotgun, Cast-iron galleries)
- **Strong SEO gap** ‚Äî architecture content is underserved vs. food/music coverage
- **High tourist volume** (18M+) with architecture-curious visitors already there
- **Photogenic and distinctive** ‚Äî great for social sharing and backlinks
- **HABS has extensive coverage** compensating for moderate Commons count
- **Narrative appeal** ‚Äî Creole architecture, preservation battles, post-Katrina rebuilding

New Orleans beats Philadelphia narrowly because of its more distinctive architectural identity and stronger tourism base. Philadelphia is an excellent #3 choice for a future round.

## Image Sources Beyond Wikipedia

### 1. Library of Congress ‚Äî Prints & Photographs Division
- **URL:** https://www.loc.gov/pictures/
- **Licensing:** No known copyright restrictions on most items; some items have specific restrictions noted. Generally public domain for pre-1928 works.
- **Quality:** Very high. Professional photography, large format scans, often 10,000+ px.
- **How to search:** Use https://www.loc.gov/pictures/search/ ‚Äî filter by collection (HABS/HAER), subject, location, date.
- **City coverage:**
  - **NYC:** Thousands of items. Extensive coverage of Manhattan landmarks, Brooklyn brownstones, bridges.
  - **New Orleans:** Excellent. French Quarter, Garden District, plantation houses extensively documented.
  - **Philadelphia:** Very strong. Independence Hall, Society Hill, industrial architecture.
  - **Washington DC:** Massive. Government buildings, embassies, residential neighborhoods.
  - **Detroit:** Good. Guardian Building, Michigan Central Station, industrial sites.
  - **Boston:** Strong. Federal-era buildings, Back Bay, waterfront.

### 2. Historic American Buildings Survey (HABS) / Historic American Engineering Record (HAER)
- **URL:** https://www.loc.gov/pictures/collection/hh/
- **Licensing:** Public domain (US Government work). Free to use for any purpose.
- **Quality:** Exceptional. Large-format photography + measured architectural drawings. The gold standard for architectural documentation.
- **How to search:** Through LOC search, filter collection to "Historic American Buildings Survey" or use browse by state/city.
- **City coverage highlights:**
  - **New Orleans:** ~800+ HABS records. One of the most documented cities.
  - **Philadelphia:** ~600+ records. Colonial and Federal architecture extensively surveyed.
  - **NYC:** ~500+ records across all boroughs.
  - **Savannah:** ~200+ records. Excellent for its size.
  - **Detroit:** ~150+ records. Key industrial and Beaux-Arts buildings.
  - **Charleston SC (bonus):** ~400+ records ‚Äî worth noting as potential future city.

### 3. Flickr Commons
- **URL:** https://www.flickr.com/commons
- **Licensing:** "No known copyright restrictions" ‚Äî effectively public domain or equivalent. Each participating institution sets terms.
- **Quality:** Variable. Ranges from snapshots to professional architectural photography. Many historical images.
- **How to search:** Search Flickr normally, filter by "The Commons" license. Or browse by institution.
- **Key institutions for architecture:**
  - Library of Congress (largest contributor)
  - New York Public Library ‚Äî excellent NYC architecture coverage
  - Boston Public Library ‚Äî Boston-specific historical images
  - Smithsonian Institution ‚Äî DC and national landmarks
  - State Archives of Florida ‚Äî Miami/Florida architecture
- **City coverage:** Best for NYC, DC, Boston. Moderate for others.

### 4. Wikimedia Commons
- **URL:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/
- **Licensing:** CC-BY-SA, CC-BY, CC0, or public domain (varies per image ‚Äî always check individual file).
- **Quality:** Highly variable. Some excellent photographer contributions, some phone snapshots.
- **How to search:** MediaSearch or category browsing. Categories like "Category:Architecture of New York City" are curated.
- **City coverage:** (see counts above) ‚Äî DC, Boston, NYC, Miami, Philadelphia lead.

### 5. Flickr Creative Commons (beyond Flickr Commons)
- **URL:** https://www.flickr.com/search/?license=2%2C3%2C4%2C5%2C6%2C9 (CC-licensed)
- **Licensing:** Various CC licenses. CC-BY and CC-BY-SA are most useful. Always verify per-image.
- **Quality:** Huge volume; quality varies enormously. Many excellent architectural photographers license CC.
- **How to search:** Flickr search with license filter. Search "[city] architecture" and sort by "Interesting."
- **City coverage:** Excellent for all major cities. NYC, LA, and Chicago have the most volume.

### 6. Unsplash
- **URL:** https://unsplash.com/
- **Licensing:** Unsplash License ‚Äî free for commercial and non-commercial use, no attribution required (though appreciated). Cannot sell unmodified photos or create competing service.
- **Quality:** High. Curated photographer community.
- **How to search:** Simple search bar. "[city] architecture" works well.
- **City coverage:** Good for popular/photogenic cities (NYC, LA, Miami, New Orleans). Weaker for smaller cities.

### 7. Pexels
- **URL:** https://www.pexels.com/
- **Licensing:** Pexels License ‚Äî free, no attribution required, similar to Unsplash.
- **Quality:** Good to very good.
- **City coverage:** Similar to Unsplash. Good for major cities.

### 8. Internet Archive (archive.org)
- **URL:** https://archive.org/
- **Licensing:** Varies. Many public domain books with architectural illustrations/photos. Check per item.
- **Quality:** Historical items can be exceptional. Scanned architectural books and periodicals.
- **How to search:** Search for "[city] architecture" in texts or images collections.
- **City coverage:** Broad but unstructured. Best for historical/vintage architecture imagery.

### 9. State/City Historic Preservation Archives

**New York:**
- NYC Landmarks Preservation Commission: https://www.nyc.gov/site/lpc/index.page
- Municipal Archives photo collection: some digitized

**New Orleans:**
- The Historic New Orleans Collection (THNOC): https://www.hnoc.org/
- Excellent photography archive. Some digitized. Check rights per item.
- Louisiana Digital Library: https://louisianadigitallibrary.org/ ‚Äî CC or public domain items

**Philadelphia:**
- PhillyHistory.org: https://www.phillyhistory.org/ ‚Äî city photo archives
- Athenaeum of Philadelphia architectural drawings collection

**Detroit:**
- Detroit Historical Society: https://detroithistorical.org/
- Burton Historical Collection (Detroit Public Library)
- Wayne State University digital collections

**Washington DC:**
- Washingtoniana Collection (DC Public Library): https://www.dclibrary.org/washingtoniana
- Smithsonian digital collections

**Savannah:**
- Georgia Historical Society: https://georgiahistory.com/
- Savannah College of Art and Design (SCAD) archives

### 10. University Architecture Photo Collections

- **MIT Libraries ‚Äî Rotch Visual Collections:** Architectural slides and photographs. Some digitized.
- **Columbia University ‚Äî Avery Architectural & Fine Arts Library:** https://library.columbia.edu/libraries/avery.html ‚Äî Massive collection, limited online access.
- **University of Virginia ‚Äî Special Collections:** Strong for Southern/Federal architecture.
- **Tulane University ‚Äî Southeastern Architectural Archive:** Excellent for New Orleans. https://library.tulane.edu/locations/southeastern-architectural-archive
- **Carnegie Mellon University:** Pittsburgh architecture documentation.
